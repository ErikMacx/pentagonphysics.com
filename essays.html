On the constants
The Emperor's New Constants
Why E = mc² and 1/137 are models, and what lies beneath them.
A century after Wigner asked why mathematics is so unreasonably effective in physics, the answer turns out to be that physics has been measuring the customer first and visiting the cupboard for clothes that fit. This essay strips the famous equations and shows the relational structure underneath.

On evaluation
The Emperor's New Benchmark
A note to be read before evaluating any paper in the Pentagon Physics programme.
The Lagrangian is a consequence, not a credential. This essay sets out the appropriate benchmark for a derivational physics programme — pre-registered predictions, kill conditions, ablation tests, and the asymmetry between answering a question and declining to address it.

Adversarial review
The Hostile Referee
A systematic stress test of Pentagon Physics.
Eighteen adversarial tests applied to the programme — nuclear mass completeness, the positive-eigenvalue rule, the alpha formula uniqueness, the bridge line, fuzzy dark matter tension, and pre-registered predictions. Five resolved cleanly, eight require more work, three present near-term kill conditions.
